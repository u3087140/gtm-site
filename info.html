2. CAN I GO TO GTM?

GTM is a licensed, all ages event. Its recommended children under 16 years of age who attend the event should be accompanied by their parent or a legal guardian. We are expecting a big excitable crowd so its probably not the best place to bring small children, toddlers and infants. We strongly suggest anyone under the age of 16 checks with their parent or a legal guardian before purchasing a ticket. NOTE TO PARENTS: It's up to you to consider whether the event is appropriate for your child. If you would like to discuss this with someone from our office please get in touch with us via: moo@gtm.net.au

3. HOW DO I BUY MY TICKETS?

7. CAN I LEAVE GTM AND COME BACK IN?

No. There are NO PASS OUTS for any GTM Event. Make sure you bring everything you need for the day in with you when you arrive.

8. CAN I BRING MY OWN ALCOHOL?

NO BYO ALCOHOL is permitted. Security will be checking persons, all bags and containers - any alcohol will be confiscated.

9. WILL IT BE OK TO SMOKE A JOINT WITH MY FRIENDS?

Definitely not! Police sniffer dogs are becoming a regular at festivals around the country. So if you don't want to get busted, booted out and miss out on all the fun, DON'T BRING ANYTHING SNIFFABLE.

10. CAN I BRING MY WATER WITH ME?

GTM is happy for you to bring in a sealed plastic water bottle or an empty plastic water bottle only to fill up at our water stations. Soft drink, juice, milk, flavoured water and sports water are not permitted.

11. CAN I BRING MY FOOD WITH ME?

Yes, food is welcome. Just make sure you pack as though youre taking your lunch to school, uni or work - you can bring along a backpack or similar size bag, but knives, eskies, picnic baskets, glass containers, picnic blankets, folding chairs and other bulky items are not permitted. Security guards will be checking persons, bags and containers and will confiscate any items that are not permitted.

12. I NEED TO BRING MY MEDICATION WITH ME, HOW DO I DO THIS?

If you need to bring in prescription medication or over-the-counter medication, you will need to make sure it is clearly labeled and preferably in its original packaging. Please be aware security may ask GTMs medical team to check your medication upon arrival. If youre unsure or have any questions, get in touch with GTM via moo@gtm.net.au

13. IS GTM ACCESSIBLE FOR EVERYONE?

Yes it is. Groovin the Moo is accessible for people with special needs including wheelchairs. GTM supports the companion program too. Please get in touch directly with GTM via tickets@gtm.net.au for specific event information in regards to access for patrons who have special needs or mobility restrictions.

14. WHATS THE DEAL WITH CAMERAS AND TAKING FOOTAGE AT GTM?

First and foremost, please respect the privacy of all other patrons, artists and service providers at GTM when using your camera or mobile phone. Professional still cameras (such as an SLR), professional video cameras (including go-pros), sound recording equipment and professional lighting and tripod set ups are not permitted at GTM without written authority from the Promoters. Any patron with equipment of this description wont be allowed to bring it in. It will be up to the patron to find somewhere to store it until the end of the event or choose to not enter the event. Small still cameras (including those with a recording function) and mobile phones are permitted for your own personal use, however: Any photos or footage taken cannot be sold or published without written authority from the Promoters. Any photos or footage taken is to be used for your personal use only  sharing it with friends via uploading it to your personal Facebook and instagram account is fine. Before you upload something to Facebook or instagram, have a think about whether youd be happy with someone else uploading that exact photo or footage of you. It might seem funny at the time, but possibly not the following day.

20. WILL THERE BE AN ATM ON SITE?

We recommend that you come to GTM prepared with cash in your wallet. ATMs will be onsite at GTM, but these are always heavily used and tend to have a bit of a queue. Best bet is to get organized BEFORE you get to GTM and bring enough cash to get you through the day. Nobody wants to stand in a line when you could be watching your favourite band.

23. WHAT IF I LOSE MY FRIENDS AT GTM?

Write down two mobile numbers one friend at the event and one adult who can help you out from home put it on your arm or a piece of paper for your pocket / bag.
